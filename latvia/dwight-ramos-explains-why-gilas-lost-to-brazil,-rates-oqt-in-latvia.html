Dwight Ramos explains why Gilas lost to Brazil, rates OQT in Latvia

06 July, 2024

After five days of positive emotions, Gilas Pilipinas was ultimately eliminated from the Olympic Games contention after losing to Brazil in the Olympic Qualifying Tournament in Riga, Latvia.

Gilas won the first quarter 22-12, sparking hope in the Philippines of another surprise in the making. Dwight Ramos was the main guy leading the team, which competed without the injured Kai Sotto. When the second half started, Gilas allowed Brazil to go on a 0-14 run, which ultimately proved to be too much to overcome.

"I think we got rushed up. They took us out of our offense, we weren't very patient. They pressured us and played very good defense. They just froze us up," Ramos explained the lackluster second half to BasketNews.

"He's a big part [of] protecting the rim, getting rebounds," the star guard said about Kai Sotto and his impact on the court that Gilas wasn't able to get on Saturday. "He's a presence on offense, too, because he's a threat down there. I think we were missing that."

Brazil took over the second half with solid defense, allowing just 6 points in the third quarter. Overall, Gilas shot the ball just 37.5% from the floor.

Before the start of the OQT, Ramos mentioned to BasketNews that the competition was supposed to be a stepping stone for the team to continue building the roster on the success of the Asian Games last October.

"For sure. I think we stepped up. We were missing Kai this game, we were missing a couple of other players but we just kept battling," Ramos said. "We beat Latvia, which was very nice, but in the end, our record was 1-2. Still a losing record for me but a very great experience here."

"I think so. We definitely need to continue with this. We're not experienced, we're not used to being this far in the tournament. [We got] a little bit frazzled in the moment. We're in the semis for the first time, and maybe we got caught up in the moment this time," the player explained.

The second half featured a plethora of rushed shots and rushed decisions. In the end, Gilas game was summarized in the end of the fourth quarter when Carl Tamayo missed a wide-open layup, which would've cut the deficit to nine points.

"It was definitely the moment of being in the semis. We just have to charge it to experience," Ramos said.

With the OQT in Riga, Latvia, now over, Gilas Pilipinas will get back to work, hoping to emulate the success they had in the tournament to advance the country's basketball in order to ultimately become a regular member of the international basketball elite.

Head coach Tim Cone has said that Gilas didn't expect to go this far. He even mentioned the team had tickets to go home after the group round.

"I think we booked our tickets [to go home] after the finals because, after our first win, we were hoping we could get that far," Ramos said with a slight smile.

The 25-year-old guard thinks that it was a positive experience overall despite losing two games in three tries.

"It was a success but we could've had more success. I would rate it maybe a B, B+," the player concluded.
