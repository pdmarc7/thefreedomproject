Metro Parks expending programs to serve more communities

23 July, 2024

NASHVILLE, Tenn. (WSMV) - July is Parks and Recreation Month and in Nashville, Metro Parks is focused on giving more people access to its resources.

Metro Parks is expanding its hours, locations and activities to help more people as the city grows.

At Smith Springs Community Center, they’re focused on helping families and young people who might not have been able to get signed up for organized programs, like summer camps and after-school activities that fill up as soon as registration opens.

The center has started new drop-in sessions and other activities to help peoples’ physical, mental and social health.

Facility manager Barbara Manuel said Smith Springs is a jewel of the community and they want everyone to have access to its fitness center, gym, pool and other activity rooms.

“The opportunities are endless,” Manuel said. “We want to be able to give families and other young people who weren’t able to get into the organized program to still be able to participate in organized activities.”

Metro Parks Director Monique Horton Odom said they have events across the county every day and have seen demand skyrocket over the past couple of years. They’re currently building new parks in areas that did not previously have greenspaces and replacing community centers to hold more activities.

“We need to grow and make sure our organization and our offerings can meet the demand of the interest that folks in the community want to see,” Odom said. “That’s a varied and diverse interest, and we stand ready to meet those.”

Odom said that includes increasing staffing to safely open up more seats in youth programs and designing other events around things people in the community want to participate in.
