IBM new cybersecurity assistant tackles threat detction and response with artificial intelligence

08 August, 2024

In response to the growing complexity and persistence of cyber threats, IBM is transforming its Threat Detection and Response (TDR) services by integrating generative AI. This advanced technology is designed to reduce the workload on security analysts by minimizing manual tasks, allowing for a more proactive and precise approach to threat management.

The newly introduced IBM Consulting Cybersecurity Assistant, powered by IBM’s watsonx data and AI platform, aims to enhance the efficiency and effectiveness of security operations. This innovative tool will be integrated into IBM’s managed TDR services, which are used by IBM Consulting analysts to optimize and streamline security measures for their clients.

The Cybersecurity Assistant harnesses IBM’s extensive AI capabilities to speed up the identification, investigation, and response to critical security threats. By combining generative AI with existing automation technologies, the system seeks to address the challenge of managing large volumes of security alerts.

According to IBM, the TDR Services can autonomously escalate or resolve up to 85% of alerts. With the addition of generative AI technologies, the remaining alerts that require human intervention can be managed more efficiently.

Key features of the IBM Consulting Cybersecurity Assistant

Accelerated Threat Investigations: The Assistant accelerates the investigation process by using historical correlation analysis, cross-referencing alerts, and enriching insights from various data sources such as SIEM, network, EDR, and telemetry. This comprehensive approach enables analysts to understand and respond to threats more accurately and quickly. The Assistant offers a timeline view of attack sequences and auto-recommends actions based on historical patterns, improving response times and reducing the time attackers can remain undetected.

Streamlined Operational Tasks: With an advanced generative AI conversational engine, the Assistant provides real-time support for operational tasks. It can automatically manage requests like ticket management, log retrieval, and command explanations. This feature not only aids in handling complex security events but also enhances overall efficiency within the Security Operations Center (SOC).

Developed in collaboration with IBM Research and based on IBM’s Granite foundation models, the Cybersecurity Assistant leverages IBM watsonx.ai and watsonx Assistant technologies. This new tool will be a crucial part of IBM Consulting Advantage, IBM’s AI services platform, delivering consistent, high-quality value to clients with improved speed and accuracy.
