Historic all-female referee crew for Bolivia-Panama

01 July, 2024

The United States was eliminated from the 2024 Copa America with a dismal 1-0 defeat by Uruguay which increased the pressure on beleaguered head coach Gregg Berhalter.

The USMNT needed to match or better Panama’s result against Bolivia with both Group C games played at the same time. And American hopes were raised when Bolivia hit back in the second half after conceding an early goal.

But moments later Uruguay scored against the USMNT in controversial circumstances, with Mathias Olivera’s tap-in allowed to stand despite the defender appearing to be in an offside position.

The USMNT failed to drag themselves back into the game and Panama went on to beat Bolivia 3-1, consigning Berhalter’s side to a humiliating early exit which could yet cost him his job ahead of the 2026 World Cup on home soil.

Regardless of the result, this Bolivia-Panama match will be a historic one in CONMEBOL's nearly 108-year history. It’s the first time a Copa America men’s game will be officiated by an all-female referee staff with Brazilian Edina Alves and assistants Neuza Back (both Brazil) and Mary Blanco (Colombia) taking charge on the pitch.

Alves fell in love with refereeing in 1999 after her friend’s father invited her to try out as an assistant referee in an amateur game, according to FIFA. For two years, she woke up at 5 am every morning to fill bags of soil at a seed nursery until she raised enough money to afford referee courses. Her career officially began in 2000, and the rest is history.

One reason why Bielsa may not have changed his team tonight is that Uruguay managed to coast their way through the first two games in this group without receiving a single yellow card. As such, only a red card tonight would put players at risk of missing the knockout round.

The USMNT, however, have Weston McKennie, Antonee Robinson and Chris Richards all on a yellow card cliff edge, meaning a caution tonight would rule them out of the quarterfinals if they qualify. Still, everyone will be pretty happy if that’s the most pressing thing on the agenda come full time tonight.

If you thought Uruguay coach Marcelo Bielsa would take things easy on the US tonight, then you clearly don’t know the Argentine very well.

His team may be virtually assured of qualification and topping the group but Uruguay are unchanged and not rotating the team.

The USMNT wanted a signature win, and they will have to beat the real deal here if they are to pull it off.

The US back line and midfield will be tasked with limiting Uruguay’s impact, and it will be interesting to see how they go about replacing Tim Weah on the right wing after his red card against Panama.

Weah provides verticality and the ability to take players on 1-on-1. There isn’t a like-for-like sub in this U.S. team, though Berhalter has options such as Haji Wright and Brenden Aaronson, and could also move Gio Reyna out to the wing and play with the 2022 World Cup midfield of Tyler Adams, Weston McKennie and Yunus Musah.
