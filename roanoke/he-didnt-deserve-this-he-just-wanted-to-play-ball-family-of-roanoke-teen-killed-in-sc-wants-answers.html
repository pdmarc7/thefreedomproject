ROANOKE, Va. (WDBJ) - A Roanoke teenager was shot and killed over the weekend in Myrtle Beach, where he was playing in a travel basketball tournament.

Jakolbi Taylor’s family says he was a rising basketball star and had hoped to make it into the professional leagues. Taylor’s aunt spoke exclusively to WDBJ7′s sister station in Myrtle Beach, WMBF, and explained through the heartbreak and grief, they are fighting for answers.

”My nephew’s dreams, aspirations will never be fulfilled,” Briana Taylor said.

16-year-old Jakolbi Taylor dreamed of playing in the NBA. A talented player for Patrick Henry High School and the Twin Hoops traveling team, Taylor explained her nephew could have gone all the way.

“He loves ball and he loves his friends, so he was here helping his team,” Taylor said. “The last game they played he put up 43 points.”

Myrtle Beach Police Department said there was a shooting around 1 a.m. Saturday at a beach access area. Jakolbi was shot and killed.

“Our lives will be forever changed,” Taylor said. “Our lives will never be the same.”

Myrtle Beach Police have not made any arrests or identified any suspects as of Monday night.

“I don’t know what happened, I don’t know what was said,” Taylor said. “Nothing should ever escalate to someone losing their life.”

Taylor is now calling for justice for the 16-year-old’s death.

“My nephew was not a gangster, he’s never held a gun in his hand,” Taylor said. “He didn’t deserve this, he just wanted to play ball. That’s it.”

Taylor explained her family will keep pushing for answers and continuing sending a message to other teens to put the guns down.

“All of these families are hurting. All of it is causing devastation,” Taylor said. “Who wins in this scenario? No one.”

Roanoke Police said they are helping Myrtle Beach Police with their investigation in any way they can.

Roanoke City Public Schools (RCPS) said there are resources available for students and families.

“We have placed Resources for Parents and Guardians under Quick Links on the Roanoke City Public School’s website should you need to have conversations about grief with your child. These can be accessed by clicking on the button for Parent/Guardian Resources near the top of the homepage next to the orange banner. Mental health services through Hazel Health are available, as well as immediate support through Carilion CONNECT at (540) 981-8181. School counselors and social workers will also be available,” RCPS told families in a robocall.
