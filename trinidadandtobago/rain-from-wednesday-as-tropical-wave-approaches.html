Rain from Wednesday as tropical wave approaches

02 July, 2024

Despite the brief reprieve from heavy rains and high winds brought by the powerful Hurricane Beryl as she made her way across the Caribbean Sea, citizens should prepare for the arrival of a fast-approaching tropical wave.

According to the Trinidad and Tobago Meteorological Service (TTMS), the axis of the wave is expected to reach the lesser Antilles by tomorrow, heralded by showers and possible high winds.

In its five-day weather outlook, the TTMS said from early Wednesday, convergence ahead of the axis’ arrival is likely to produce cloudiness, with showers over varying areas, across the windward islands and the southern leeward islands.

At the peak of the approaching wave, the TTMS noted an area of disturbance, dubbed Invest AL96, which is being monitored.

The development of the system into a tropical cyclone, has been reduced to a 20 per cent chance over the next 48 hours and a 30 per cent hance over the next seven days.

The country’s likely to experience a few partly cloudy periods with light or moderate showers, and a low to medium chance for the development of an isolated heavy shower or thunderstorm, from early morning to mid-afternoon.

The weather agency said the following day is likely to see partly cloudy to at times cloudy periods, with light or moderate showers in some areas, and a medium chance for the development of an isolated heavy shower or thunderstorm. These conditions are expected from the early morning to early afternoon – courtesy a combination of low-level convergence and moisture advection from the southeast.

On Friday, there’s a reduced likelihood of the heavier showers expected to land mid-week.

The forecast’s a low to medium chance of an isolated heavy shower or thunderstorm developing during the morning to mid-afternoon, as a ridge of high pressure is expected to develop with mostly fair conditions across the lesser Antilles.

The TTMS said convergence at the southern extremities of the ridge, across Trinidad and Tobago and the southern windward islands, is likely to produce partly cloudy patches, with a few light or moderate showers, interrupting fair conditions in some areas.

Saturday is going to see some moisture, with conditions forecast to be fair with partly cloudy patches producing brief showers in some areas.

Weak low-level convergence and patches of moisture advection, create an environment for a low chance of an isolated heavy shower or thunderstorm to develop during the late morning or afternoon.

Sunday is when the rains are likely to pick up again, as the TTMS predicted a noticeable increase in low level convergence and moisture advection across Trinidad and Tobago and the windward islands.

These conditions are likely to produce partly cloudy to cloudy periods with showers over varying areas, plus a medium to high chance for isolated heavy showers and thunderstorm activity.

The axis of another tropical wave is located in the eastern equatorial Atlantic near 21W.

Before the arrival of the rains tomorrow, however, a surge in Saharan dust is expected from this evening.

This increase will be interrupted by the expected rains associated with the tropical wave, reducing concentrations to mild to occasionally moderate, but only until the evening when concentrations rise again to mostly moderate.
