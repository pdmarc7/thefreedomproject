Jalen Wilson wins MVP as other Jayhawks put together solid NBA Summer League performances

23 July, 2024

Over the past two weeks, KU fans had the opportunity to enjoy watching six former KU basketball players compete in the 2024 NBA 2K24 Summer League in Las Vegas. Headlining the group was the Brooklyn Nets' Jalen Wilson, who the NBA named Summer League MVP just before the championship game. 

Wilson played in all five of the Nets' summer league games, averaging 21.8 points per game, shooting 47.3 percent from the field, 55 percent from three on eight attempts per game and 89.5 percent from the free-throw line. He led Brooklyn to a 3-2 record, highlighted by a 33-point, 10-rebound double-double performance against the Orlando Magic in which Wilson had the game-winning bucket, putting the Nets up 102-100 for the win. 

In his first year in the NBA, Wilson appeared in 43 games, averaging 5.0 points in 15.5 minutes per contest. His performance in the Summer League could show he's ready for a heavier load on his shoulders as the Nets continue their rebuild. Wilson said his goal this summer was to show how he's improved and how things are slowing down for him.

"Just compete, play hard, learn as much as I can," Wilson said. "Game-by-game, hopefully, it keeps slowing down... I feel like that first year is so fast for a rookie… So hopefully, the second year slows down, and I will get to learn the game."

In addition to Wilson, Johnny Furphy was another former Jayhawk wing who had the chance to get a lot of playing time in Las Vegas. He was there with the Indiana Pacers, who selected him in the second round of the draft. 

Furphy struggled a bit shooting the basketball in the summer league and knocked down just 37 percent of his shots from the field and 28.6 percent of his shots from behind the arc.. However, he showed flashes of great play, especially in an 18-point performance against the Suns. In that game, Furphy also sunk a dagger three with less than 20 seconds on the clock to seal the Pacers victory. 

Furphy ended his summer league campaign after four games, averaging 14 points, 6.0 rebounds and 1.8 steals per game. 

The Toronto Raptors boast a duo of former Jayhawks in 2023 13th overall pick Gradey Dick and Ochai Agbaji, who was drafted 14th overall by the Cleveland Cavaliers in 2022. After being traded by the Cavaliers, Agbaji played the 2022 season and half the 2023 season for the Utah Jazz before being traded to Toronto. Agbaji played in four games in Las Vegas and averaged 6.0 points, 4.8 rebounds and 1.5 assists.

Dick appeared in just two Summer League games before missing time due to a high ankle sprain. In that pair of games, he averaged 11 points, 7.0 rebounds and 3.0 assists per game. Dick began his rookie year with a rocky start, at one point playing a handful of G-League games. But Dick was able to turn around the year and finished the season averaging 8.5 points, 2.2 rebounds and 1.1 assists.

A 2021 graduate, Marcus Garrett played in four games for the Charlotte Hornets. In 20.7 minutes per game, he averaged 8.5 points, 3.8 rebounds, 1.5 assists and 1.5 steals. Garrett played for the Hornets G-League affiliate team, the Greensboro Swarm, during the 2023-24 season.

Parker Braun went undrafted in 2024 but joined the Denver Nuggets for the Summer League, the same team his brother Christian Braun was drafted to in 2022. While Christian's summer league days are behind him, Parker played in two games, averaging 1.0 point, 4.0 rebounds and 2.0 assists.
