5 Ways To Earn Air New Zealand Airpoints Without Leaving The Ground

06 July, 2024

Summary

  • Air New Zealand offers various ways to earn Airpoints, including flights, banks and credit cards, gas stations, car rentals, and hotel partners.
  • Air New Zealand has partnerships with banks such as ANZ, Kiwibank, Westpac, and American Express to earn Airpoints.
  • Retail partners, such as supermarkets, hardware stores, and real estate companies, also offer opportunities to earn Airpoints.

While many still believe you need to fly to earn frequent flier miles, there are now many ways that airlines are looking to lure you in and gain your loyalty through several earning privileges with businesses on the ground. Air New Zealand is no different, with a growing network of Airpoints partners. Based at Auckland International Airport (AKL), New Zealand, it has an increasing network of destinations across the Pacific Rim.

Air NZ provides more options for trans-Tasman flights than any other airline, with nine Aussie destinations on offer from the City of Sails (Auckland). The carrier also has an extensive network in the Pacific Islands, including unique spots like Niue (the Rock of the Pacific) and Tonga, so you can redeem your miles to a relaxing beach in no time.

Are beaches not your thing? That's okay, as the carrier connects New Zealand to some of the largest cities on the globe, including Tokyo, Shanghai, and New York.

At home, the carrier is the most significant carrier by destinations served, with 20 domestic ports across the country. With a fleet of regional ATR72 and Airbus A320/A321 configured aircraft, these planes connect passengers from as far north as the Bay of Islands / Kerikeri (KKE) to the most southerly controlled airport in the Commonwealth, Invercargill (IVC).

So, if you want to jump onboard an Air New Zealand flight soon, how can you do so without even leaving the ground? Let's explore how to earn Airpoints without even saying the word 'airport.' $1 Air New Zealand Airpoints Dollar exchanges to NZ $1 ($0.61) when purchasing an Air New Zealand flight on their website.

This article will look at ways to earn Air New Zealand Airpoints for New Zealand residents.

1 Banks and credit cards

ANZ, Kiwibank, Westpac and American Express

While airline-specific credit cards are the most common way to add airline miles to your balance, Air New Zealand is no different. The carrier has partnered with four other banks and credit cards to provide an easy way for passengers to add more to their Airpoints pot. Australian banks ANZ and Westpac are by far the largest banks in New Zealand, and they offer Visa (ANZ), and Mastercard (Westpac), as a way for their customers to earn more.

Kiwibank, a locally owned bank, also offers Air New Zealand Airpoints through its low-rate or platinum credit cards. You can earn up to one Airpoint for every NZ $115 ($70) to NZ $200 ($122) spent. Most banks also allow an 'Airpoints' advance, where you can redeem up to $200 Airpoints dollars before you've earned them.

American Express is the only other credit card where earning Airpoints is an option, and they provide by far the best earning rate of one airport for every NZ$59 ($36) spent; however, this will decrease to NZ $70 ($46) come October.

2 Gas stations

Z Energy & Caltex

While touring around the Kiwi countryside, you can earn points at your local petrol/gas station. In New Zealand, two providers let you fill and earn. Earning rates at both Caltex and Z Energy is one point for every 100 liters purchased. How many gas tanks would you need to buy to book the new Economy Bunk Beds coming to Air New Zealand this year? 3 Car rental

Apex, Avis, and Budget

Traveling domestically and abroad allows you to earn Airpoints. Air New Zealand has partnered with three of the major car rental companies in New Zealand to allow its loyal customers to earn points. Avis and Budget can be found at most airports worldwide. At the same time, Apex Car Rental, historically a Kiwi company now sold to the Avis Budget Group, has a network of city and airport depots and the most prominent tourist hotspots across the country.

  • Book via www.airnewzealand.co.nz/rental-cars, and you can earn up to three Airpoints dollars for every day of your rental

4 Hotel partners

11 to 'check-in'

Resting your weary head is a top place to earn Airpoints dollars. The Star Alliance carrier, Air New Zealand, has partnered with 11 hotel chains with various earning privileges. All have differing earning rates, and these providers include:

  • Swiss Belhotel International
  • IHG Hotel & Resorts
  • Millennium Hotel & Resorts
  • Commodore Hotel
  • Pacific Resort Hotel Group
  • Marriott Bonvoy
  • Langham Hospitality Group
  • Quest Apartment Hotels
  • Accor Live Limitless
  • Fable Hotels and Resorts
  • Jetpark Hotels

5 Retail partners

Shop till you drop

From Cape Rienga to Stewart Island (top of NZ to the bottom), plenty of retail partners enable Airpoints customers ample opportunities to gain extra points through their daily spending. Some of the most recognizable businesses include:

  • New World (Supermarket)
  • Mitre10 (Hardware store)
  • Smith City (Home goods store)
  • Spark (Mobile)
  • Liquorland
  • Bayleys (Real Estate)
  • Audi
  • Gilmours and Trents (Food wholesale)
  • Partridge Jewelers
  • Baby on the move
  • Petdirect
  • AIA
  • Aelia Duty-Free
  • Bluebridge ferries

Many of these providers have different earning rates and bonuses depending on what you spend or purchase. For more detail, check out the stores website.
