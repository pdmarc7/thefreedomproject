Maldives Of America: 7 Best Sandbars In The US

06 July, 2024

Summary

  • Surprisingly, there are many Maldives-like sandbars in the US that look and feel like a tropical island atoll.
  • Torch Lake near Rapid City in Michigan is one of the most stunning US sandbars with white sand and turquoise waters, earning it the nickname the "Maldives of Michigan".
  • Florida Peanut Island Sandbar in West Palm Beach and Three Rooker Island in Clearwater are among the most beautiful sandbars in the USA that give off tropical Maldives vibes.

Sandbars are naturally formed by the forces of waves or water currents that carry sand or sediments onto the beach over time—submerged but partially exposed. These formed ridges of sand are more exposed when it's low tide and become popular lounging points for tourists, especially on sunny days.

The world is dotted with otherworldly landscapes, and beaches are no exception—with sandbars as the crowning features. Indonesia, the Philippines, Thailand, and the Maldives are among the world's top destinations for beaches and sandbars.

A visit to Sumilon Island, one of the best things to do in Oslob, Cebu in the Philippines, gives travelers a chance to experience its pristine, white-sand beaches and gorgeous sandbar. And, of course, the Maldives, famous for its amazing islands, beaches, and sandbars, is perfect for a magical summer escape.

Still, as these gorgeous destinations take center stage, the US is also not to be messed with when it comes to beaches and sandbars. Americans don't have to venture into foreign lands to experience Maldives-like sandbars, either. Florida and Hawaii, for instance, are a match, as are certain other (and rather surprising) destinations across the country, like Michigan. Picture the crystal-clear blue waters, white-sand beaches, and stunning sandbars—yes, the USA has many of these, just like tropical paradises do!

Even America's most beautiful coastal towns are among the top places to visit if you're looking for gorgeous sandbars that look and feel like a tropical Maldives oasis, especially now that summer is already in full swing. Here are some of the most stunning places with the best sandbars in America (crystal-clear turquoise waters included)—and cocktails too!

7 Peanut Island Sandbar, West Palm Beach, Florida

Located on a 79-acre island with a beautiful sandbar in the Palm Beach Inlet area

Gleaming with clean and clear waters, rich marine life, a beautiful beach, and a sandbar, Peanut Island Sandbar breathes new life into its humble beginnings as a stack of sand in the early 20th century. Today, the island is one of the top tourist destinations in Florida for beachgoers and adventurers. Its location on the Intracoastal Waterway between West Palm Beach and Riviera Beach makes it more inviting for locals and tourists to explore its pristine sandy beaches.

The sandbar in the north of the island is where the spotlight is—dotted with boats and beachgoers who want to go snorkeling, boating, or simply enjoy basking in the sun (with a delicious drink in hand)! The vistas are also not to be missed, with the Blue Heron Bridge, home to some of the best scuba diving in Florida, Port of Palm Beach, and stunning marinas in view.

Look out for sea turtles, manatees, colorful tropical fish, and many more. Visitors might even see them on the 10-minute boat ride there!

There are tour operators that offer boat trips to Peanut Island, including Sandhill Charters and Cruisin Tikis. The Peanut Island Shuttle Boat leaves the mainland every 25 minutes and suggests advance booking for tickets, although walk-ins are welcome on a first-come, first-served basis.

6 Three Rooker Island, Clearwater, Florida

A semicircular island with a sandbar, surrounded by pristine, turquoise waters

Three Rooker Island is also one of the best places in Florida that feel like the Caribbean—and for good reason! The sandbar, which was formed due to erosion, has just emerged in the last decade and has been one of the top tourist destinations in the Sunshine State, giving off tropical Maldives vibes.

Three Rooker Island is semicircular, but it changes from time to time due to the behavior of the water currents. It features white-sugary-sand beaches, crystal-clear blue waters, and a dramatic flora and fauna, which is why it was designated as a wildlife sanctuary.

Three Rooker Island is only accessible by boat as it's separated from the mainland, but there are tour operators who take travelers to the island, where people can enjoy snorkeling, fishing, bird-watching, and swimming.

Bay Excursions and Seabreeze Boat Rentals are among the tour operators in the area.

5 Torch Lake Sandbar, Rapid City, Michigan

Billed as the "Maldives of Michigan", known for its crystal-clear turquoise waters white-sand beaches, and tropical-like sandbar

The crystal-clear turquoise waters and white sandy beaches are the top reasons why Torch Lake Sandbar is billed as "the Maldives of Michigan". The sandbar is accessible by boat or kayak, but during low water levels, you can enjoy swimming or even walking to get to it, since it's only a few yards from the shore.

One of the clearest lakes in the Midwest, Torch Lake encompasses 19 miles of picturesque landscapes and ecological resources, including the sandbar where visitors enjoy swimming and basking in the sun. The lake is the second-largest in Michigan and is also a haven for anglers and watersports enthusiasts.

Torch Lake Tiki is a food boat on the Torch Lake Sandbar, where visitors can enjoy great food and drinks, as well as water equipment rentals. Other boat tour operators are Brook Walsh, Torch Lake Boat Rentals, and Play North Watersports, among others.

4 Haulover Sandbar, Miami Beach, Florida

Known for its calm, clear blue waters and sandy bottom

Calm, clear turquoise waters and sandy—these are the Haulover Sandbar's crowning features that beckon many travelers to explore this beautiful Maldives-like sandbar in Miami Beach. The sandbar sits west of the marina at Haulover Beach, one of the nicest beaches the locals prefer in Miami.

New visitors will find many spectacular things to do at Haulover Sandbar, including swimming, lounging on the sand, island hopping, and fun watersports. There are parties every weekend at the sandbar, too, especially in the summer, where visitors flock to enjoy the clear blue waters.

Haulover Sandbar is also accessible by boat, jet ski, canoe, or kayak since it's located on a sand shoal that emerges at low tide. Several tour operators also offer boat rentals and packages for travelers who want to explore the sandbar. If you have a boat, even better!

Atlantic Watersports, Haulover Sandbar Boat Rental, and Haulover Sandbar Cruise & Day Trip are among the best tour operators in the Miami Beach area.

3 Crab Island, Destin, Florida

An award-winning sandbar known for its emerald waters and vibrant atmosphere

Destin's hottest spot, Crab Island, radiates a festive atmosphere and a lot of fun in the water. Crab Island boasts tons of epic activities to try that'll make you feel like you're on vacation in the Maldives, such as snorkeling, wake surfing, dolphin tours, swimming, and, of course, basking in the sun!

A beautiful submerged sandbar, Crab Island is flocked by many tourists in the summer, but it's also a year-round destination for its crystal-clear emerald waters and sandy bottom. It's located on the south side of Choctawhatchee Bay and is one of the most photographed spots in Florida.

It's also been aired on national TV, including HGTV and Good Morning America, as well as in many travel magazines. Access to this award-winning sandbar is by boat, jet skis, and pontoon, among others.

Tour operators in the area include Destin Vacation Boat Rentals, Crab Island Company, and more.

2 Whale Harbor Sandbar, Islamorada, Florida

Known for its shallow water and a popular spot for beach picnics

Located in Islamorada, one of the best islands in the Florida Keys with a small-town vibe, Whale Harbor Sandbar is a crowning feature on the island, with amazingly white sand and crystal-clear waters that look almost exactly like the Maldives' beaches.

Located less than a mile from the shore, the sandbar is a popular spot for water and beach lovers, with spectacular things to do like kayaking, boating, paddleboarding, and many other watersports.

The sandbar has shallow water, which is perfect for putting lounge chairs, picnic tables, umbrellas, and grills for a spectacular beach picnic. Like many other Maldives-like sandbars in the US, Whale Harbor Sandbar is accessible by boat or canoe.

Clearly Unique Charters, Miami Boat Cruises, and Key Largo Adventures are among the best tour operators in the area.

1 Kaneohe Bay (Ahu o Laka) Sandbar, Oahu, Hawaii

Also known as Ahu o Laka, with incredible views of the Koolau Mountains

One of the best things to do in Kaneohe on the island of Oahu, a quiet Oahu gem, is a trip to Kaneohe Bay Sandbar, a submerged shoal that is more exposed at low tide and features crystal-clear turquoise waters and white sand. The sandbar is about three miles long and has amazing views of the Koolau Mountains, which were seen in the movie Jurassic Park.

The sandbar was known as Ahu o Laka, meaning Altar of Laka, the God of Hula, and is accessible by boat or kayak. Snorkeling, swimming, island hopping, and many other watersports activities are popular at the sandbar. Keep an eye on sea turtles and colorful tropical fish!
