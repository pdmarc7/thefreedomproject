Standard Bots: Robotics Automation Company Closes $63 Million

23 July, 2024

Standard Bots, a robotics automation leader, announced it has raised $63 million in total funding. Its recent Series B round was led by General Catalyst and participated in by Amazon Industrial Innovation Fund and Samsung Next. Lachy Groom, Quiet Capital, 468 Capital, Lightscape Partners, and Box Group provided additional venture capital support.

The company has already successfully launched the RO1 robotic arm and is now preparing to bring its new AI-powered robots to market, unlocking use cases and industries untouched by traditional robotics like complex assembly, food preparation, or dishwashing.

Standard Bots designs and assembles collaborative robotic arms in the US to transform industrial sectors like manufacturing. And the company is also transforming a transformer-based model that utilizes the power of AI to enhance robotic capabilities. By observing human demonstrations, Standard Bots enables robots to master intricate tasks from loading complex welding fixtures to folding clothing with unprecedented precision, marking a pivotal moment in the industry and unlocking a realm of new possibilities previously deemed by many to be beyond robotic reach. Standard Bots’ RO1 and RO2 robotic arms are currently in production and are designed for high variability and challenging real-world applications, a stark contrast to traditional, pre-programmed robots that often cannot navigate unpredictable conditions like dynamic or cluttered environments and unexpected obstacles.

From industry giants like NASA and Timken to small businesses, Standard Bots’ goal is to help companies enhance productivity, improve operational efficiency, significantly reduce costs, and address the growing labor shortage in manufacturing. And unlike legacy robotics solutions, Standard Bots’ intuitive software is as easy to use as a smartphone. With no coding expertise required, anyone can program its robots, from seasoned professionals to newcomers.

This democratization of robotics fosters innovation but also encourages diverse contributions, pushing the boundaries of what robotics can achieve. And for more customized applications, customers can develop their own apps on Standard Bots’ platform using flexible APIs and modern technologies like React.

As part of this funding round, Max Rimpel of General Catalyst will join the company’s board alongside existing board member and lead seed investor Itai Tsiddon. And the new funding round will be used to expand Standard Bots’ engineering team, accelerate the development of its series of next-generation robots in new sizes, and enhance the proprietary platform that makes these robots intuitive for everyday use.
