On Armenia’s Constitution Day, Pashinyan Again Calls for New Constitution

05 July, 2024

Claiming that the current Armenian Constitution does not “express” the views of the lay Armenian citizen, Prime Minister Nikol Pashinyan again called for the adoption of a new constitution on Friday—Armenia’s Constitution Day.

Throughout his tenure as prime minister, Pashinyan has made passing remarks for amending the constitution, as it relates to the system of governance in Armenia.

It was not until last year that Pashinyan began to advance the theory that if the constitution is not amended and maintains some of its current wording, it would not threaten peace and stability in Armenia and the region. He argued that the reference made in the Constitution’s Preamble on the reunification of Armenia and Armenia, which is a quote from Armenia’s Declaration of Independence, did not correspond to the current geopolitical realities.

Pashinyan and his allies have since also advocated for not only changes to the Constitution, but Armenia’s Coat of Arms and National Anthems.

It could be said that Pashinyan’s statement sparked a new demand by President Ilham Aliyev of Azerbaijan, who has preconditioned the signing of a peace treaty with Yerevan to changing of Armenia’s constitution. Aliyev and other high-ranking Azerbaijan officials claim that Armenia’s Constitution contains territorial claims from Azerbaijan.

By calling for removal of sections of the constitution that reflect Armenia’s historical and national aspirations, Pashinyan is tacitly agreeing to —if not echoing—Baku’s concerns.

“Our Constitution is is in deep socio-psychological conflict” with the understanding of sovereignty, Pashinyan said Friday in his statement on Armenia’s Constitution Day.

“Due to circumstances known to all of us, the citizens of the Republic of Armenia today do not believe that the Constitution expresses their ideas and agreements on the rules of their life with their neighbors, with their community, with other residents of his state. The citizens believe that the ruling elite created this text, announced its adoption and, in fact, introduced it in Armenia,” Pashinyan said.

“I was and remain convinced that this is a fundamental problem for our country, and our country needs a new Constitution, and now I am talking not so much about a new text, but about a new method of creating and enacting it. We need a new constitution which the people will consider to be what they created, what they accepted and believe that what is written in it is their idea of the state they created and the relations between people and citizens in that state,” he added, emphasizing his belief that the document needs to have an “organic” connection to the people.

“There is now a lot of discussion around this topic both within the country and abroad. However, we must steadily continue the path of strengthening our state – the Republic of Armenia, and make it invulnerable in institutional, psychological and physical terms,” Pashinyan continued.

“This is both a difficult and honorable path, and we are on the right track. However, in order to take this path, we need to focus on a specific task: to serve the interests of the Republic of Armenia as a state. Because the interest of the Republic of Armenia is the interest of its sovereign—the the people of the Republic of Armenia. The interest of the real people living in the real Armenia, who created the Republic of Armenia as a tool to ensure their freedom, well-being, happiness, security, to create a fair environment on their internationally recognized territory, and we must not deviate from this goal,” emphasized Pashinyan.
