A new milestone for bitcoin hardware wallets

08 August, 2024

Commercially available Bitcoin hardware wallets are marking a significant milestone by reaching their tenth anniversary. The first hardware wallet, the Trezor Model One, was launched on July 29, 2014, and since then, the industry has grown into a competitive market featuring brands like Ledger, SafePal, Keystone, and Coldcard. Ledger's CTO, Charles Guillemet, noted that their devices now secure over 20% of the overall crypto market, with the industry projected to reach a value of $1.6 billion by 2029.

Trezor, co-founded by Pavol "Stick" Rusnak and Marek "Slush" Palatinus, began as a hobby project in response to the demand for secure Bitcoin storage solutions that didn't rely on exchanges. Stick recalled the early days, when users who understood Bitcoin's potential lacked the technical skills for secure self-custody. The first prototypes were built on Raspberry Pi and featured additional boards with a display, buttons, and a USB port. Overcoming challenges in hardware design, Trezor's first commercial devices quickly sold out, indicating strong market demand. Despite initial doubts, Trezor was able to expand production without relying on venture capital funding.

The original Trezor Classic was priced at 1 Bitcoin (BTC) with a Metallic version costing 3 BTC, equivalent to tens of thousands of dollars by today’s standards, though the devices originally cost $100-$300 each. Stick emphasized Trezor’s commitment to open-source standards, fostering a collaborative ecosystem that has benefitted the broader crypto community. As hardware wallets evolve, they face new challenges, including increased regulatory scrutiny and the importance of education in navigating these complexities. Stick noted that governments are attempting to regulate Bitcoin and intimidate developers but believes in the continued relevance of self-custody solutions.

Veronica Wong, CEO of SafePal, and Lixin Liu, CEO of Keystone Wallet, both highlighted the evolution of hardware wallets towards mainstream adoption, emphasizing the importance of education and innovation in making crypto more accessible and secure. The journey of hardware wallets over the past decade demonstrates the resilience and innovation within the Bitcoin community, with these devices playing a crucial role in the evolution of the broader crypto market. While many hardware wallets now support multiple cryptocurrencies, there is a growing trend toward Bitcoin-only firmware to enhance security.
