'Biggest race of my life:' Local motocross racer prepares for national championship in TN

22 July, 2024

A local motocross racer is soon heading to Tennessee in hopes of being crowned the fastest in the country.

Seven-year-old Maverick Novak is getting ready to race against 41 other kids in his age group for a national championship.

“This is the biggest race of my life,” Novak says.

In late July, Maverick Novak will travel to Hurricane Mills, Tennessee for the Monster AMA Amateur National Motocross Championship.

Maverick says he’s been locked onto making nationals this year.

“I’ve been training for this whole thing to make it to nationals.”

That training includes plenty of racing and even physical training to stay at peak performance.

“When you get used to it, it’s easy, but I think when you start, it gets hard.”

Novak’s Dad says Maverick has put in the work necessary to make it to Tennessee.

“I’m very proud. The kid puts his heart and soul into it. There are good days. There are bad days. He always gets right back up and keeps on charging no matter what the conditions are,” says Brandon Novak.

Brandon says he hopes this early success is a long-term lesson for Maverick.

“Hopefully, it’s a lesson for him the rest of his life. He sticks with it and realizes if you work hard great things will come.”

Maverick will leave for Tennessee on Friday, July 26th, and the competition begins July 29th.

The winner will be decided by who has the best average time from all the races that week.
