06 July, 2024

Africa, Europe, Algeria and Italy "are pillars of a development of food security that looks at and addresses the major problems and resolves them in a common logic of commitment". The Minister of Agriculture, Food Sovereignty and Forestry said this today in Algiers, Francesco Lollobrigida, at the formalization ceremony of the agreement between Bf International Best Fields Best Food Ltd, a company controlled by the Italian group Bf Spa, the Algerian Ministry of Agriculture and Rural Development and the Agricultural Investment Fund for the concession of 36.000 hectares in the wilaya ( southern Timimoun province. The project involves the creation of an integrated seed-finished product supply chain totally developed and managed by Bf Spa with the collaboration of local institutions.

“Today is a special day, because we sign this formal act which enables a large Italian company like Bonifiche Ferrarsi to create and consolidate a strong partnership between two nations,” said the minister. The objective of the group led by Federico Vecchioni is to deliver a "turnkey" to Algeria an entire complete agri-food supply chain, from the supply of seeds to the industrial production derived from crops, including pasta. The project, aimed at Algerian agri-food self-sufficiency, involves the use of advanced agronomic techniques, such as crop rotation to optimize agricultural yield, and according to Algerian sources has a total estimated value of 420 million dollars. “An investment that guarantees food self-sufficiency as a commitment, which guarantees product quality and which can also guarantee another element: the protection of the environment. Because farmers are the first environmentalists on the planet and where there is agriculture there is defense of the territory”, added Lollobrigida.

“I am happy that this process is entitled, as far as Italy is concerned, to a person, Enrico Mattei, who supported Algeria in that desire to be free and to be strong, helping it as an African nation should be helped, considering it a partner with equal rights, equal duties and with great respect,” added Lollobrigida, in reference to the Italian government's Mattei Plan for the development of Africa. In Algeria, Enrico Mattei is widely recognized as playing a very high role of support, friendship and closeness during the years of the national liberation war (1954-1962). Mattei has historically supported both the National Liberation Front and the Provisional Government of the Algerian Republic, to which he provided a significant contribution in the negotiations of the Evian Agreements. His name also evokes the large number of Algerian students, future executives and managers of the oil and energy industry, trained on his initiative in the Eni schools in San Donato Milanese. “Before Enrico Mattei, the European peoples looked at Africa with predatory logic that we do not want to ever assert again anywhere. We believe that Africa should not be treated with a charitable vision that puts at ease the consciences of some inhabitants of countries considered more developed”, added Lollobrigida.

The agreement signed today in Algiers represents Bf Spa's largest international project "in the Mediterranean and for the Mediterranean" he said Federico Vecchioni, CEO of BF Spa, at the formalization ceremony of the agreement between Bf International Best Fields Best Food Ltd, a company controlled by the Italian group, the Algerian Ministry of Agriculture and Rural Development and the Agricultural Investment Fund for the concession of 36.000 hectares in the southern wilaya (province) of Timimoun. The plan involves the creation of an integrated seed-finished product supply chain totally developed and managed by Bf Spa with the collaboration of local institutions. “Italy and Algeria continue on a common journey. From this afternoon, our technicians will prepare all the actions to be ready for autumn sowing", added the number one of Bf Ferraresi. Once fully operational, the cultivation of cereals - durum and soft wheat - (approximately 70 percent of the surface) and the remaining part dedicated to legumes - lentils, beans, etc. The productions will be destined for the local market.
