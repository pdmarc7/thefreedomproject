When Granit Xhaka signed for Arsenal in 2016, he looked like he had been moulded precisely to fit the role of a deep-lying playmaker.

The single midfield pivot with the stylish left foot, he could have been lab-grown it looked so natural for him.

But not many imagined he could, or would, change genres so extensively throughout his career. The last two years have proven that theory incorrect.

Despite a revival at Arsenal, Xhaka was allowed to move to Bayer Leverkusen last summer in a deal that could rise to €25million (£21.2m; $27m), where he became metronomic, with possession flowing through him.

Switzerland look to Xhaka to progress the ball. The last time he did not start an international game was November 2021 — a run that an abductor injury had threatened to end today.

But Murat Yakin declared his captain fit and this tournament can now continue to show Xhaka at his most well-rounded.

England need to ensure they are braver on the ball today. There have been far too many backwards and sideways passes in the middle third so far this tournament — which is such a crucial area for ball progression.

The new formation should help, but trusting each other to receive the ball in tight areas is equally as key.

Phil Foden has another opportunity to show everyone just how good a player he is. But much of that may depend on how the plan comes together.

Here is Foden, speaking to BBC Sport pitchside ahead of kick-off:

💬 “For me, it’ll be more central, staying more inside, what I prefer anyway — I like to be in there. So hopefully it’ll suit me a bit better today.

“If the team plays well, I think the attacking forwards play well as well. If we get the formation right and the press right, it’ll give us a lot of confidence and we’ll play a lot better.

“We’ve worked hard on (the formation). In training, it’s been working really well. I’m looking forward to putting the practice to work and hopefully getting rewards from it.”

And if you were wondering about the England motivations ahead of the game — Gareth Southgate’s 100th — then Foden added:

💬 “I feel a little bit we want to win for him, the big occasion that it is. So we definitely want to win for him.”

It says something that it’s Switzerland who can stick to their shape and XI, and they will be confident of repeating against England, what they did to Italy.

In Aebischer and Rieder, they have two lively attacking wide threats, while Vargas was exceptional in the last 16.

Can Gareth Southgate find the gaps in this plan that Luciano Spalletti’s Italy could not?

So then, this is what we are expecting from England in terms of shape. It’s going to be key how involved Saka and Trippier can be in attacking moves, rather than getting stuck in predominantly defensive duties — certainly as a pair.

Let’s see if that also frees up some space for Bellingham and Foden to drift, while Mainoo and Rice will have a lot of space to control.

This shape seems specifically designed to deal with Switzerland’s formation and patterns, which is interesting in itself.
