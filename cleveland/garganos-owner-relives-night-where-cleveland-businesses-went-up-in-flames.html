Gargano’s owner relives night where Cleveland businesses went up in flames

22 July, 2024

CLEVELAND, Ohio (WOIO) - Multiple Clark-Fulton neighborhood businesses went up in flames in a blaze that hospitalized a Cleveland firefighter, Lt. Mike Norman confirmed.

One of the businesses was a restaurant belonging to WWE Pro Wrestler Johnny Gargano’s father called Gargano’s Restaurant and Catering.

Firefighters were called to the scene just after 9 p.m. on July 21, according to Lt. Norman.

Flames were quickly spreading throughout the West 25th Street and Marvin Avenue one-story building, which had four storefronts, including a restaurant and event hall, said Lt. Norman.

Lt. Norman stated, “crews were making good progress until a water main break depleted the water supply to both hydrants in use, forcing additional companies to relay water from distant hydrants.”

Firefighters lost water pressure from both hydrants they were hooked up to on West 25th Street, Lt. Norman described.

“They were having a hard time, especially when it flared up,” said owner Frank Gargano. “Then it just went through the whole building.”

Lt. Norman said firefighters were forced to hook up to distant hydrants from separate water mains.

Additional crews were called to the scene to help with hose relays, said Lt. Norman.

Nearly 70 firefighters spent three hours battling the blaze because the delay allowed the fire to rekindle, according to Lt. Norman.

Part of the roof then collapsed and brick parapets along the roof line fell, stated Lt. Norman.

Firefighters successfully protected the businesses on both sides of the building, one of which was a gas station, Lt. Norman said.

However, the building the fire ravaged is a total loss with an estimated $700,000 in damage, Lt. Norman stated.

“I been here 52 years, this was my life,” said Frank Gargano.

One firefighter suffered a back injury, Lt. Norman confirmed, and he was stable while being taken to MetroHealth Medical Center.

The American Red Cross of Northern Ohio helped the “exhausted” firefighters on scene, according to Lt. Norman.

Frank told 19 News this wasn’t the way he wanted to say goodbye. He said he was planning to sell the building and was supposed to sign the papers on Tuesday. Now, he says the only thing he can do is have it torn down.

“Everyone knocks this neighborhood but its not true they’re good people,” said Frank.” Thank you all, it’s been a lot of years”.

19 News inquired about the hydrants, to which the City of Cleveland Division of Water responded on July 22, “Cleveland Fire Engine 20 flushed and inspected the hydrants at the intersections of W 25th St and Marvin Ave and W 25th St and Daisy Ave on May 14, 2024. No issues were reported.”

As for the water main break the water department says a crew is currently onsite repairing the break. The main will be analyzed after the area is excavated during the repair process.
