The city of Cincinnati's Office of Environment and Sustainability announced the launch of the official website for its initiative, "Electrify Cincy."

A collaborative effort between city government and the community, Electrify Cincy aims to educate neighbors and contractors as the city works toward the Green Cincinnati Plan's goal.

Included in that goal is to reduce carbon emissions by 50% by 2030 and to have 20,000 electrified homes by 2028.

Building electrification involves the shift from powering buildings with fossil fuels to powering them with electricity to reduce costs and improve the environment.

"Electrifying buildings gives us the best opportunity for each and every person to do their part to fight climate change as our electricity grid transitions to renewable sources. The biggest step that any one person can take is turn their efforts toward shifting the sources that power our buildings away from fossil fuels. We can take this step towards a healthier future for our planet when we commit to considering electrification," sustainability manager at Cincinnati OES Molly Robertshaw said.

For more information on Electrify Cincy and its goals, visit the official website.
