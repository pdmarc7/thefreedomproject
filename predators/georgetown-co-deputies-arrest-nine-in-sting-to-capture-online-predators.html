Georgetown Co. deputies arrest nine in sting to capture online predators

22 July, 2024

GEORGETOWN COUNTY, S.C. (WCSC) - A multiagency sting has led to the arrests of nine men who are accused of seeking to exploit and abuse children through online platforms, according to the Georgetown County Sheriff’s Office.

Georgetown County Sheriff Carter Weaver announced Monday that the sheriff’s office in partnership with the South Carolina Attorney General’s Office Internet Crimes Against Children Division led to the capture of the individuals.

“Operation Tidal Wave,” which began in February, targeted individuals who were attempting to establish inappropriate relationships with minors online. During the investigation, the suspects were arrested after they tried to meet with the victims they believed were children.

Weaver said some of the suspects even shared pornographic photos online and brought drugs to the meetups.

These nine people were arrested in this part of the operation:

  • Thomas Jarrod Hood, 32, of Myrtle Beach, is charged with distribution of methamphetamine, solicitation of a minor, criminal sexual contact with a minor or attempt, victim aged 11 to 14 years and dissemination of obscene material to a person under 18 years of age.
  • Nicardo Melbourne Vassell, 35, of Myrtle Beach, is charged with failure to stop for blue light, solicitation of a minor and criminal sexual contact with a minor or attempt, victim aged 11 to 14 years.
  • Kevin John Richmond, 51, of Myrtle Beach, is charged with solicitation of a minor, first-degree sexual exploitation of a minor, criminal sexual contact with a minor or attempt, victim aged 11 to 14 years and dissemination of harmful material to minors.
  • Jason Alexander Morgan, 25, of Calabash, N.C., is charged with solicitation of a minor and criminal sexual contact with a minor or attempt, victim aged 11 to 14 years.
  • Mark E. Tilghman, 62, of Surfside Beach, is charged with solicitation of a minor and criminal sexual contact with a minor or attempt, victim aged 11 to 14 years.
  • Nicholas James Russo, 31, of Myrtle Beach, is charged with solicitation of a minor and attempted sexual exploitation of a minor, victim aged 11 to 14 years.
  • Robert Edward Calloway, 48, of Myrtle Beach, is charged with solicitation of a minor and criminal sexual contact with a minor or attempt, victim aged 11 to 14 years.
  • Julio Francisco Gomez Sauzo Apellidos, 31, a foreign national, is charged with solicitation of a minor and criminal sexual contact with a minor or attempt, victim under 16 years of age.
  • Weunder Inai Segura Lopez, 34, of North Myrtle Beach, is charged with solicitation of a minor and criminal sexual contact with a minor or attempt, victim aged 11 to 14 years.

“The sad reality is that I am not surprised by the number of predators that were charged, but I am sickened nonetheless by how arrogant and willing they were to victimize our children,” Weaver said in the release.

Sheriff Weaver thanked the following agencies for the joint effort to arrest the suspects: the Federal Bureau of Investigation, Homeland Security, the Naval Criminal Investigative Service, the US Secret Service, the U.S. Attorney’s Office, the Charleston County Sheriff’s Office, the Horry County Sheriff’s Office, the Mount Pleasant Police Department, the Marion County Sheriff’s Office, Myrtle Beach Police Department, and the South Carolina Department of Corrections.
